People, emotions

Portraiture is all about the face. It is where photographers take a rehearsed and carefully decided image of a person’s distinctive facial features and expressions, while capturing the person’s attitude, personality, and identity as well. The photograph’s emphasis is the person’s face, but that does not mean it will no longer include the person’s body or even a blurred background. It can be close-up images, body portraits, and etc.

When taking portraiture, the photographer must ensure that the subject’s face is sharp and focused, especially his or her eyes. The subject’s pose should be flattering. The photographer can also try to lighten the mood, for example, by telling a joke, in order to capture the person’s natural expressions.
